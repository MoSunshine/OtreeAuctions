In the following, you can participate in up to 100 second-price sealed-bid auctions. In each auction you bid against another bidder. The role of the other bidder is taken by a computer.

The bidder who places the lower bid wins. The price is equal to the second-lowest bid. If both bidders place the same bid, each of them wins in 50 percent of the cases.

Before each auction your costs are drawn from a random distribution. The lowest cost you can have is $100 and the highest cost you can have is $200. If you win, your profit is equal to the difference between price and your costs. If you do not win, your profit is $0.
