In the following, you can participate in up to 100 English auctions. In each auction you bid against another bidder. The role of the other bidder is taken by a computer.

The price clock starts at a price of $200. Each second the price decreases by $1. The bidder who drops out last wins. The price is equal to the price that was displayed on the price clock when the first bidder dropped out.

Before each auction your costs are drawn from a random distribution. The lowest cost you can have is $100 and the highest cost you can have is $200. If you win, your profit is equal to the difference between price and your costs. If you do not win, your profit is $0.
